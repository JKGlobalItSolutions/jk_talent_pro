NEET 2024

NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is a written medical entrance test organized by the National Testing Agency (NTA). It’s for students who want to get into MBBS, BDS, or AYUSH courses in India. NEET used to be called AIPMT (All India Pre-Medical Test) in the past. Every year, a large number of students take the NEET exam to qualify for admission to recognized medical colleges across the country. NTA uses the scores from the NEET-UG 2024 exam to create a list of the best-performing students. About 1.75 lakh students will be able to get into medical colleges through NEET.

Before you start preparing for NEET 2024, it’s important to understand the basics, like ‘what is NEET 2024 exam?’, expected NEET 2024 exam date, paper pattern of NEET 2024, NEET form date 2024, NEET syllabus 2024, eligibility criteria for NEET 2024, NEET 2024 registration etc. This article has all the information you need to know.

What is the purpose of NEET exam?

The NEET exam aims to provide a single, national-level entrance test for students who wish to pursue medical programs like MBBS, BDS, nursing, and AYUSH courses in India. NTA has been organizing NEET (UG) since 2019 with approval from the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare and the Ministry of Education, as directed by the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India.

A successful performance in NEET allows students to secure seats in both private and government medical institutes across India. NEET has replaced multiple state-level and national-level entrance tests, including AIIMS and JIPMER, following the “One Nation One Test” policy, streamlining the admissions process for medical programs.

About NEET exam

  • The NEET 2024 exam is essential for students aiming to study medicine (MBBS, BDS, etc.) in India or abroad.
  • It’s a written test that covers the subjects from classes 11 and 12, consisting of 200 multiple-choice questions, with 50 questions in each subject.
  • To be eligible for the NEET 2024 exam, candidates need to have completed their Class 12 education with majors in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
  • The minimum age requirement is 17 years, or candidates should turn 17 by December 31, 2024. There are no restrictions on the upper age limit or the number of attempts for the NEET 2024 exam.

NEET 2024 Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility and Qualifications for NEET 2024

Before you apply for NEET 2024, it’s crucial to understand the requirements:

NEET 2024 Eligibility Criteria

What is the age requirement for NEET 2024 examination?

Candidate should be at least 17 years old at the time of admission or reach that age by December 31 of the admission year for the undergraduate medical course.

Nationality

Eligible candidates can be Indian citizens, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs), Persons of Indian Origin (PIOs), Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs), or foreign nationals.

What is the academic qualification for NEET 2024?

Candidates should have either passed or be appearing for the Class 12 board examination or its equivalent.

Which subjects are necessary to take up NEET?

As per NMC and DCI regulations, the candidates must have passed in Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English individually. Additionally, the candidate needs to have a minimum of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology combined in the qualifying exam. The candidate also need to secure a rank in the NEET (UG) – 2024 merit list for admission to undergraduate medical courses.

What percentage is needed in Class 12 to appear for NEET 2024 exam?

For the General category, candidates need a minimum of 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. For SC, ST, and OBC-NCL categories, a minimum of 40% is required in the qualifying examination.

These criteria are essential to be checked before applying for the NEET 2024 exam.

What Is New in NEET 2024 exam? – Expected Changes in NEET 2024

Candidates planning to take up NEET exam 2024 can expect a few changes in the upcoming NEET-UG exam since NTA has introduced several changes in the past 2 years.

Some changes introduced earlier were as follows:

  • The overall exam duration was increased by 20 minutes in NEET 2024 to a total of 200 minutes (03:20 hours) to be held from 02:00 PM to 05:20 PM (IST).
  • Changes in the exam pattern with introduction of section A and section B (having optional questions).
  • Qualifying NEET UG exam is a must even for Indian candidates who desire to pursue medicine abroad.
  • No restriction on the number of attempts at NEET or any upper age limit. However, minimum age limit restriction must be followed as per corresponding categories.
  • To seek admissions in AIIMS and JIPMER institutes, qualifying NEET is a must.
  • Scores of NEET shall be accepted for female candidates who wish to pursue nursing programmes at AFMS institutes.
  • Added and allocated more exam centres, both in India and abroad (14 cities abroad).
  • Examination fee was revised.

NEET 2024 Answer keys

The official authorities typically release the NEET 2024 answer keys on their official website about 2-3 weeks after the exams have taken place. However, students can get an early idea of their performance by referring to unofficial websites for the answer keys. Additionally, OMR response sheets will be made available.

Initially, a provisional set of answer keys is published for students to review. They have the option to challenge the answer keys if they find any discrepancies. These challenges are then assessed by NTA officials, and if found to be accurate, corrections are made accordingly. After making corrections, a final set of answer keys is released, which is considered the definitive set of answers.

How to Calculate NEET 2024 Score?

Here’s a simplified version of the steps to calculate your NEET score:

    • First, compare your answers in the OMR sheet with the official answer keys when they are released.
    • Make sure the question paper code on both the OMR sheet and answer key matches. You can find the code at the top of both pages.
    • Calculate your NEET score by adding 4 marks for each correct answer and keeping track of incorrect answers.
    • If you marked more than one answer for a single question, consider it as incorrect and assign zero marks to it.

NEET 2024 Score = [{4 * Number of correct responses} – {1 * Number of incorrect responses}]

Factors Deciding NEET exam Cut-off

NTA declares the NEET cut-off for various categories annually, and it varies each year due to different considerations. The determinants influencing the NEET cut-off include:

  • Total students appearing for NEET exam
  • Availability of seats
  • The difficulty level of the NEET exam question paper
  • Marking Scheme and NEET cut-off trends from previous years
  • Reservation policy of the government as per category

NEET 2024 Counseling

NTA reveals NEET results a few weeks after the NEET exam concludes, marking the beginning of the counseling process. The Medical Counseling Committee (MCC) oversees counseling for the 15% All India Quota (AIQ), while counseling for the remaining 85% state quota seats is managed separately by state counseling authorities. Only candidates who have cleared the NEET cut-off for that year are eligible to apply for the counseling process. Aspirants must register online to participate in counseling.

In medical colleges, 15% of MBBS and BDS seats are reserved for the All India Quota, and the remaining 85% are designated as State Quota. According to the Medical Council of India, there are approximately 845 medical colleges offering 1,03,877 seats based on NEET-UG scores across the country.

NEET 2024 Preparation tips

How to Prepare for NEET 2024?

Initiating NEET exam preparation requires a well-defined strategy and proper guidance. The foundation involves understanding the fundamentals of NEET, including the exam pattern, syllabus, and marking scheme for NEET 2024. The question paper for the NEET 2024 exam will be crafted from the Class 11 and 12 NCERT syllabus, covering the subjects of Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.

Here are some tips that can help you get started with your NEET preparation for 2024:

  • Create a Timetable: Develop a study timetable that allocates equal time to all subjects according to your convenience.
  • Master NCERT Content: Gain a strong command over the NCERT syllabus. It forms the core of NEET preparation.
  • Note-Taking: Create your own notes while studying. This aids in better retention of information.
  • Understand Chapter Importance: Identify crucial topics and understand the weightage of chapters for each subject. Prioritize your focus accordingly.
  • Emphasize Conceptual Clarity: Particularly in Physics, focus on achieving a clear understanding of concepts.
  • Utilize Previous Years’ Papers: Refer to previous years’ question papers to familiarize yourself with the exam pattern and question types.
  • Reference NCERT and Other Resources: Consult not only NCERT books but also related resources to address a variety of questions.
  • Practice with Sample Papers and Mock Tests: Regularly solve sample papers and take mock tests to gauge your preparedness.
  • Doubt Clarification: Seek clarification for any doubts you may have and analyze your performance frequently.
  • Expert Guidance: If needed, seek guidance from experienced individuals or coaching experts.
  • Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle: Ensure a healthy lifestyle by consuming nutritious food and getting adequate rest. A balanced lifestyle positively impacts your ability to focus and learn effectively.

How to get 650+ in NEET 2024 exam?

The key to excelling in NEET lies in a thorough understanding of NCERT content, requiring a detailed knowledge of each line with no room for shortcuts, tricks, or substitutes. Additionally, achieving a score of 650+ in this highly competitive test necessitates not just hard work but also strategic and intelligent efforts. While challenging, it is certainly an attainable goal with the right approach.

Here’s a checklist to help you plan and score more than 650+ in NEET 2024 for admission to your dream medical college:

1. Understanding the Purpose of NEET

Start by cultivating a strong passion and dedication for achieving success in the NEET exam with an excellent score.

2. Creating a Well-Structured Plan

Develop a thoughtful timetable that covers all subjects, giving equal attention to each. Every chapter holds significance, and each mark contributes to your overall rank.

3. Grasping the Exam Format and Marking System

Answer 180 questions to score a total of 720 marks. Attempt 45 questions from each section – Physics, Chemistry, Botany, and Zoology. To reach a score of 650, aim for at least 163 correct answers.

4. Mastering the NEET Syllabus

Thoroughly review all topics in each chapter; don’t overlook any. Solve chapter-wise and topic-wise questions after understanding the concepts.

5. Recommended NEET Study Materials

Utilize NCERT as the primary resource to achieve a score of 650 or more in NEET. Master all concepts before moving on to related practice questions from other sources for each subject.

6. Emphasizing Practice

Practice solving numerous questions during your preparation to confidently target 170 correct answers in the main exam. Be cautious of negative marking. Solve sample papers, previous years’ question papers, and take daily mock tests.

7. Utilizing Learning Tools

Create mind maps and diagrams for better retention. Given the vast syllabus, use tools like flowcharts, diagrams, mind maps, mnemonics, etc., to enhance learning efficiency.

8. Analyzing Previous Years’ Question Papers

Examine patterns in previous years’ papers to identify repeated questions, recurrent concepts, and important chapters. Seek guidance from seniors or teachers if needed.

9. Regular Performance Evaluation

Consistently assess and analyze your performance. Celebrate achievements and take note of weaker areas, putting in extra effort to improve in those areas.
